About Me

I graduated with a dual Master's degree in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology from the Technical University of Munich (TUM, Germany) and the École Nationale Supérieure de l'Électronique et de ses Applications (ENSEA, France). My academic journey began with digital systems, signal processing, and embedded systems, before progressively evolving toward deep learning and computer vision.

I further strengthened this specialization during an exchange at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST, South Korea), and through my Master's thesis at BMW Group in Munich, where I worked on computer vision and model optimization for autonomous driving applications.

Research Interests

Efficient Deep Learning

Design deep neural networks that are both high-performing and computationally efficient for deployment in real-world scenarios. Balancing accuracy with resource constraints is especially critical for embedded systems and edge devices.

Robust AI Systems

Advancing trustworthy and robust AI by developing models resilient to adversarial attacks and capable of continual learning, crucial for ethical and fair deployment in sensitive domains such as medicine

3D Vision & Generative Models

3D vision, diffusion models, and generative approaches hold strong potential for applications in medical imaging and autonomous systems. My research explores these cutting-edge technologies.

Model Compression

Focus on compression techniques such as quantization, pruning, and structural reparameterization—leveraging feature map and latent space analysis to streamline inference without compromising accuracy.

Open Paper

HiFi-LLP: High-Fidelity, Low-Cost Latency Predictors

IEEE SOCC 2025 • BMW Group Collaboration

HiFi-LLP proposes a fast and accurate latency estimation framework for deep neural networks without hardware execution. It achieves high fidelity through layer-wise calibration while maintaining low computational cost, enabling rapid architecture search and deployment on embedded platforms.

View Paper on IEEE →

RepFC: Universal Structural Reparameterization

CVPR 2025 Mobile AI Workshop

RepFC introduces a flexible reparameterization technique that unifies multiple network structures into a single efficient inference model. It enhances deployment performance across different hardware accelerators and supports plug-and-play optimization of convolutional backbones.
