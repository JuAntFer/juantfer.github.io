Research Interests

I am particularly driven by the challenge of designing and optimizing deep neural networks that are both high-performing and computationally efficient for deployment in real-world scenarios. As models become increasingly complex to achieve state-of-the-art results, the need to balance accuracy with resource constraints becomes critical—especially for embedded systems and edge devices.

My interest lies in applying compression techniques such as quantization, pruning, and structural reparameterization by analyzing feature maps and latent spaces, in order to streamline inference without compromising performance, while also improving other key metrics such as accuracy. In this context, I am also deeply interested in the challenge of improving network robustness—particularly in developing models that are resilient to adversarial attacks and capable of learning from new data without forgetting previously acquired knowledge, all while avoiding access to the original training dataset. These issues are especially pressing in scenarios where data privacy, storage capacity, and computational resources are limited.

I hold a dual Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology from TUM (Germany) and ENSEA (France), with an exchange semester at KAIST (South Korea), where I focused on AI and robotics.
