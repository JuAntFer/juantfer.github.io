About Me

I am passionate about advancing deep learning at the edge—designing efficient models for real-world deployment on embedded systems and resource-constrained hardware. My research focuses on compression techniques such as quantization, pruning, and structural reparameterization to optimize performance while reducing computational cost.

These methods are key to enabling state-of-the-art AI in applications like autonomous driving, healthcare, and smart devices, where power, latency, and memory are limited.

I hold a dual Master’s degree in Electrical Engineering and Information Technology from TUM (Munich) and ENSEA (Paris), with an exchange at KAIST (South Korea) focused on hardware-aware AI.
